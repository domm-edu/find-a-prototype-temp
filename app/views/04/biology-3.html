What is an A Level?

A levels are subject-based qualifications usually assessed by exams. You usually choose 3 subjects to study but you can take more or fewer.

You typically study them full time over 2 years, after you've finished GCSEs.

Course overview

You will build on your first year of study into a wide range of exciting and challenging topics all at the cutting edge of present-day scientific research. A few of these topics include stem cells, drugs and their effects, the study of ecosystems and the effect of man on the environment.
