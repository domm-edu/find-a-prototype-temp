What is an A Level?

A levels are subject-based qualifications usually assessed by exams. You usually choose 3 subjects to study but you can take more or fewer.

You typically study them full time over 2 years, after you've finished GCSEs.

Entry requirements

Entry into the Sixth Form requires six passes at GCSE Level (Grades 9-5) including English and at least a Grade 4 in Maths. This course requires GCSE Grades 6 - 6 in Combined Science or GCSE Grades 6 and 6 in Biology and at least one other science. To do more than one science, a GCSE Grade 7 is required in at least one science. A GCSE Grade 6 in Maths is required.

What can I do if I don't meet these?

Course overview

The format for A-level Biology has recently changed to a two-year linear course. The two-year course comprises eight units, four in each year. There is no longer an ISA associated with this course, instead students must complete a series of assessed practical activities throughout the year and be prepared to answer questions on these in the final exams. These exams are sat at the end of the course and are no longer modular.
