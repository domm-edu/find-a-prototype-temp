If you don't meet the entry requirements for the course you're interested in, don't panic. There are lots of things you can do.

Talk to your school or college

If you've only just missed your grades, try talking to your school or college. They might be flexible on what theyâ€™ll accept.

Resit any necessary exams

You can resit GCSEs the following year. You can also take autumn exams in GCSE English language and maths.

Your sixth form or college might allow you to do resits alongside your new course.

Look for other courses

Not all sixth forms and colleges have the same entry requirements. So look around to see if there are other courses you could get onto.

Consider an apprenticeship

Apprenticeships combine training with a practical job. Their entry requirements are often lower than courses.

Get advice

You could talk to someone you trust, like a parent, carer, teacher or careers adviser.

You can also talk to the National Careers Service (NCS) for free.
